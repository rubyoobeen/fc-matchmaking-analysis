Does squad power decide the match before kickoff?

An analysis of season cards, squad grade, and win rate across hundreds of thousands of EA FC Online matches — looking for where the game rewards spending, and where skill still wins out.

Finding 01

Squad strength gap predicts who wins — up to a point

Team A vs Team B, by average squad grade

Every match was split into a stronger and weaker side by average squad grade. As the gap between the two sides grows, the stronger side's win rate climbs steadily — from an even 40% at a near-zero gap up to roughly 60% once the gap reaches about five grade points.

This holds firmly across 300,000+ matches. Beyond a five-point gap, match volume drops into the hundreds and the trend becomes too noisy to trust — so the conclusion is intentionally bounded to the range the data actually supports.

A bigger squad-grade advantage does translate into more wins — but the effect is gradual, not dominant. Even at the largest reliable gaps, the stronger side still loses roughly 4 in 10 matches.
Finding 02

One player's card grade, alone, barely matters

Individual Sp Grade: winners vs. losers

Comparing the average card grade of every player on a winning side against every player on a losing side shows almost no difference. A single player's own grade is a weak predictor of whether their match is won — what matters is how their grade compares to the squad they're facing, not the number on its own.

This lines up with an earlier, independent finding from this project's first pass: season version (which card — TOTY, ICON, base — not just its grade) explains far more of the variation in performance than grade does by itself.

Finding 03

Premium seasons cluster at the top of the win-rate table

Win rate by season type, sample-size filtered

Ranking every season by win rate — after filtering out low-sample seasons that aren't statistically trustworthy — shows premium and special-edition cards (TOTY, ICON TM, KLeague, Heroes, New Generation) disproportionately near the top of the list.

Looking at individual high-volume players across their own different seasons tells the same story at a personal level: the same player's win rate shifts depending on which card they're using — evidence that card version, not raw stats alone, shapes outcomes.

Finding 04

The most-played squad members are also the most recognizable

Top players by appearance count, win rate included

Ranking individual players by how often they appear across the captured matches turns up a familiar list of names — Son Heung-min, Lee Kang-in, Lúcio, Luke Shaw, Dani Carvajal, Karim Benzema, Achraf Hakimi, and other widely recognizable real-world talent. Pick frequency tracks closely with public name recognition, more so than with any single in-game performance metric in the dataset.
